# Dev Portfolio

Christopher Meyer's personal developer portfolio. A rebuild of the previous site
(`old-dev-portfolio`) with the same core content but a revamped look and feel. It
serves two audiences from one site, leading with the engineer identity.

## Language

**Recruiter**:
A person evaluating Christopher as a software engineering hire (internships,
full-time). The primary audience; the homepage leads to them.
_Avoid_: employer, hiring manager (use these only when the distinction matters)

**Client**:
A local business owner who might hire Christopher for freelance web/software work.
The secondary audience, served by the dedicated freelance page.
_Avoid_: customer, buyer

**Homepage** (`/`):
The engineer-facing portfolio: hero, projects, experience, skills, contact. Kept
clean and impressive for Recruiters; carries a clearly signposted door to the
freelance page for Clients.

**Freelance page** (`/freelance`):
The Client-facing page holding Services & Pricing, and the future home of the
rebuilt examples gallery. Reached via the hero's "Need a website?" door.

**Two doors**:
The hero's pair of primary calls to action that split the two audiences: a
"hiring?" path (stays on the homepage / resume) and a "need a website?" path (to
the freelance page). The structural expression of the builder-first, two-path stance.

**Featured Work**:
The homepage section showcasing the three flagship projects (Site Builder,
StafferAI, PlaySkillSphere). The canonical name for the projects showcase.
_Avoid_: Portfolio, Projects, Work (used loosely on the old site)

**Examples gallery**:
The set of fictional industry-specific demo sites used as freelance sales assets.
Removed from this rebuild for now; to be reintroduced under the freelance page later.
_Avoid_: templates, demos (the route was `/examples` on the old site)
